The question under the product

You have built a platform that earned a 19.5% reduction in in-person visits inside the FLI cohort and scaled to 10,600 monitored patients across 169 nursing homes during the pandemic. The CIMA+ model, DH Link, DH Forms, the Eurecat-backed Rehabilify launch in January, and Lina Health are all live. Three offices on three continents. Under thirty people. The question is not whether the product works. The question is which of your current AI bets deserves the next three engineer-weeks, and which ones are better served by simpler tools than the ones you are reaching for.

Where we work

Eduba works in the layer above tooling. We call it computational orchestration. Roughly sixty percent of the problems teams reach for AI to solve are traditional code and database problems. Roughly thirty percent are rule-based logic. About ten percent are genuine AI problems, where a model is the best instrument available. We suspect at least one of your current AI workstreams is sitting in the wrong ten percent, and that a proper read would redirect engineering time toward the one that deserves it. Rehabilify, Lina Health, and the CDTI-funded predictive models are each plausible candidates. A week of structured review would name the answer.

What a scoped engagement looks like

For Feeld we ran a scoped sprint: workshop, advisory sessions, an organizational context architecture, and a strategic operations framework tailored to their product and team. Feeld used the output to pick which AI bets the team would actually fund and which ones would wait. The same shape of engagement, scoped for Doole's stack, would cover Lina, the CDTI predictive work, and the DH Link integration layer.

On the regulated side

Our paper Ethics Engine is a psychometric assessment tool for evaluating ideological and moral patterns in LLMs. It gives clinical AI teams an audit instrument before hospital ethics committees or MDR-aligned regulators ask for one. You will need that instrument for Lina Health inside twelve months. Better to ship it on your timetable than on someone else's. Paper: arxiv.org/abs/2510.11742. Repo: github.com/RinDig/AuditEngine.

On production ML

Eduba partners with NLP Logix for work that sits below the orchestration layer. NLP Logix has been in machine learning since 2011 and runs over 150 data scientists, so if the CDTI predictive-models effort needs production data pipeline work, that route is already there.

La pregunta bajo el producto

Han construido una plataforma que logró una reducción del 19,5% en visitas presenciales dentro de la cohorte FLI y escaló a 10.600 pacientes monitorizados en 169 residencias durante la pandemia. El modelo CIMA+, DH Link, DH Forms, el lanzamiento de Rehabilify con Eurecat en enero, y Lina Health están todos en producción. Tres oficinas en tres continentes. Menos de treinta personas. La pregunta no es si el producto funciona. La pregunta es cuál de sus apuestas de IA actuales merece las próximas tres semanas de ingeniería, y cuáles se resolverían mejor con herramientas más simples que las que están eligiendo.

Dónde trabajamos

Eduba trabaja en la capa por encima del tooling. La llamamos orquestación computacional. Alrededor del sesenta por ciento de los problemas para los que los equipos recurren a la IA son problemas de código y base de datos tradicionales. Alrededor del treinta por ciento son lógica basada en reglas. Cerca del diez por ciento son problemas genuinos de IA, donde un modelo es el mejor instrumento disponible. Sospechamos que al menos una de sus líneas de trabajo de IA está en el diez por ciento equivocado, y que una lectura adecuada redirigiría tiempo de ingeniería hacia la que lo merece. Rehabilify, Lina Health y los modelos predictivos del CDTI son candidatos plausibles. Una semana de revisión estructurada nombraría la respuesta.
